18// The full game of partnership spades.
19// See https://dkmgames.com/CardSharp/Spades/SpadesHelp.php
20// This is played by four players in two partnerships; it consists of a bidding
21// phase followed by a play phase. The bidding phase determines the contracts
22// for the play phase. The contract consists of:
23// - Each player bidding how many tricks they can take.
24// - If a player bids 'Nil' (meaning '0'), then they have a special condition
25// for points
26// based on whether they can avoid taking any tricks.
27//
28// There is then a play phase, in which 13 tricks are allocated between the
29// two partnerships. Each partnership gains 10 times their combined contract
30// if the partners are able to collectively take at least as many tricks as that
31// combined contract, otherwise the partnership loses 10 times their combined
32// contract.
33//
34// Any tricks taken in excess of a partnership's combined contract are worth 1
35// point and considered a 'bag' - for every 10 bags collected over the course of
36// the game, the partnership is penalized 100 points.
37//
38// In the case of a Nil bid, if that partner avoids taking any tricks during the
39// round, the partnership gains a 100 point bonus. Conversely, if that partner
40// takes any tricks, the partnership will lose 100 points (but these tricks
41// still count toward the other partner's contract).
42//
43// The action space is as follows:
44// 0..51 Cards, used for both dealing (chance events) and play;
45// 52+ Bids (Nil, 1-13) used during the bidding phase.
46//
47// During the bidding phase, every player will have 1 turn for making a bid.
48// During the play phase, every play will have 13 turns for playing a card.

32// Reconnaisance Blind Chess - imperfect information version of chess, where
33// players do not see the full board, but they can make explicit "sensing" moves
34// to reveal specific parts of the board.
35//
36// Specifically, based on [1,2] the implementation follow these rules:
37//
38// - The rules of Standard Chess apply, with the exceptions and modifications
39// that follow.
40// - A player cannot see where her opponent's pieces are.
41// - Prior to making each move a player selects a 3 x 3 square of the chess
42// board. She learns of all pieces and their types within that square. The
43// opponent is not informed about where she sensed.
44// - If a player captures a piece, she is informed that she made a capture (but
45// she is not informed about what she captured).
46// - If a player's piece is captured, she is informed that her piece on the
47// relevant square was captured (but she is not informed about what captured
48// it).
49// - There is no notion of check or mate (since neither player may be aware of
50// any check relationship).
51// - A player wins by capturing the opponent's king.
52// - If a player tries to move a sliding piece through an opponent's piece, the
53// opponent's piece is captured and the moved piece is stopped where the
54// capture occurred. The moving player is notified of the square where her
55// piece landed, and both players are notified of the capture as stated above.
56// - If a player attempts to make an illegal pawn attack or pawn forward-move or
57// castle, she is notified that her move did not succeed and her move is over.
58// Castling through check is allowed, however, as the notion of check is
59// removed.
60// - There is a "pass" move, where a player can move nothing.
61//
62// There are some differences to the original rules [1,2]:
63//
64// > "All rules associated with stalemates or automatic draw conditions are
65// > eliminated"
66//
67// Automatic draws are made after the same board repeats itself 3 times
68// in the game (kNumRepetitionsToDraw) or after a large number of moves
69// (kNumReversibleMovesToDraw). This is to make sure that random play
70// in the game would stop after a limited number of steps.
71//
72// > "Turn phase: [..] if the opponent captured a piece on their turn,
73// > the current player is given the capture square"
74//
75// Turn phase is eliminated: all the capture information can be deduced as the
76// difference of player's pieces observations in the sensing phase.
77//
78// > "Sense phase: the player chooses any square on the chessboard to target
79// > their sensor. Then, the true state of the game board in a three square by
80// > three square window centered at the chosen square is revealed to the
81// > sensing player."
82//
83// Sensing is done as picking a sensing window that fully fits within the
84// chessboard and is not centered on an underlying square. The centering can
85// make the sensing window smaller (as a rectangle near the border of the
86// chessboard) which gives strictly less information than a better placed window
87// (that remains a full square). This modification eliminates strategically
88// useless sensing actions.
89//
90// > "If the move was modified [..] then the modified move is made, and
91// > the current player is notified of the modified move in the move results."
92//
93// All the modifications can be deduced through the observations provided
94// by the observation tensors or strings and are not given explicitly.
95//
96// [1] https://rbc.jhuapl.edu/gameRules and
97// [2] https://reconchess.readthedocs.io/en/latest/rules.html
98//
99// Parameters:
100// "board_size" int Number of squares in each row and column (default: 8)
101// "sense_size" int Size of the sensing square.
102// "fen" string String describing the chess board position in
103// Forsyth-Edwards Notation. The FEN has to match
104// the board size. Default values are available for
105// board sizes 4 and 8.

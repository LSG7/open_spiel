15// This game is a simplified version of the Sheriff of Nottingham board
16// game, as introduced in [1].
17//
18// Game dynamics
19// =============
20//
21// Player 1 (the "smuggler") selects the number of `num_items` (0 or more)
22// illegal items to be placed in the cargo. The selected number is unknown to
23// Player 2 (the "sheriff").
24//
25// Then, the game proceeds for `num_rounds` bargaining rounds. In each round,
26// the following happens:
27//
28// - The smuggler selects an integer bribe amount, in the range 0 to `max_bribe`
29// (inclusive). The selected amount is public information. However, the
30// smuggler does *not* actually give money to the sheriff, unless this is the
31// final round.
32// - Then, the sheriff tells the smuggler whether he is planning to inspect the
33// cargo. However, no cargo is actually inspected other than in the final
34// round. The sheriff can change his mind in later rounds, except for the
35// final round.
36//
37// Payoff computation
38// ------------------
39//
40// At the end of the game, the payoffs of the players are computed as follows:
41//
42// - If the sheriff did *not* inspect the cargo, the smuggler gains a payoff
43// equal to `num_items * item_value - bribe_amount`, and the sheriff gets a
44// payoff equal to `bribe_amount`, where `bribe_amount` is the *last* bribe
45// amount.
46// - If the sheriff inspects the cargo, and no illegal items were present, the
47// smuggler gains a payoff equal to `sheriff_penalty`, while the sheriff loses
48// `sheriff_penalty` value.
49// - Finally, if the sheriff inspects the cargo and finds `num_items` (1 or
50// more) illegal items, the smuggler loses a total value computed as
51// `-num_item * item_penalty`, while the sheriff gains value `num_items *
52// item_penalty`.
53//
54//
55// Game size
56// ---------
57//
58// +-------+-------+--------+-----------------+----------------+----------+
59// | Max | Max | Num | Num sequences | Num infosets | Terminal |
60// | bribe | items | rounds | pl 0 | pl 1 | pl 0 | pl 1 | states |
61// +-------+-------+--------+--------+--------+-------+--------+----------+
62// | 3 | 3 | 1 | 21 | 0 | 5 | 4 | 32 |
63// | 3 | 5 | 2 | 223 | 73 | 55 | 36 | 384 |
64// | 3 | 3 | 3 | 1173 | 585 | 293 | 292 | 2048 |
65// | 3 | 5 | 4 | 14047 | 4681 | 3511 | 2340 | 24576 |
66// +-------+-------+--------+--------+--------+-------+--------+----------+
67// | 5 | 3 | 1 | 29 | 13 | 5 | 6 | 48 |
68// | 5 | 3 | 2 | 317 | 157 | 53 | 78 | 576 |
69// | 5 | 5 | 3 | 5659 | 1885 | 943 | 942 | 10368 |
70// +-------+-------+--------+--------+--------+-------+--------+----------+
71//
72//
73//
74// Game parameters
75// ===============
76//
77// "item_penalty" double Penalty (per item) incurred by the smuggler
78// for carrying illegal goods (default = 2.0)
79// "item_value" double Value of each successfully smuggled item
80// (default = 1.0)
81// "sheriff_penalty" double Sheriff's penalty for inspecting a cargo
82// that does not contain illegal items
83// (default = 3.0)
84// "max_bribe" int Maximum bribe amount, per round
85// (default = 3)
86// "max_items" int Maximum numbers of items that fit the cargo
87// (default = 3)
88// "num_rounds" int Number of bargaining rounds (default = 4)
89//
90// References
91// ==========
92//
93// If you want to reference the paper that introduced the benchmark game, here
94// is a Bibtex citation:
95//
96// ```
97// @inproceedings{Farina19:Correlation,
98// title= {Correlation in Extensive-Form Games: Saddle-Point Formulation
99// and Benchmarks},
100// author= {Farina, Gabriele and Ling, Chun Kai and Fang, Fei and
101// Sandholm, Tuomas},
102// booktitle={Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems
103// (NeurIPS)},
104// year={2019}
105// }
106// ```
107//
108// [1]:
109// https://papers.nips.cc/paper/9122-correlation-in-extensive-form-games-saddle-point-formulation-and-benchmarks.pdf
